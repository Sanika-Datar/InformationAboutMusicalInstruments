Information about Tabla





Tabla is a percussion instrument most commonly used in North Indian Classical Music. It consists of two drums called Bayan (left) and Dayan (right) as per the hand they are most commonly played with. The drums consist of a layer of goatskin stretched over a metal or a clay vessel. The left and right vessels are shaped differently, with the right being narrow and more cylindrical. It is used as both solo and an accompanying instrument.



The head of each drum has a central area of "tuning paste" called the Syahi. Syahi is common in many drums of Indian origin. This method allows these drums to produce harmonic overtones and is responsible for their unique sounds. Syahi is constructed using multiple layers of paste made from starch (wheat or rice) mixed black powder of various origins. The precise construction and shaping of this area is responsible for modification of drums natural overtones, resulting in clarity of pitch.

Some of the famous Tabla players include Zakir Hussain, Pandit Anindo Chatterjee, Pandit Anokhelal Mishra, Ustad Sukhwinder Singh Namdhari and Ahmed Jan Thirakwa.
